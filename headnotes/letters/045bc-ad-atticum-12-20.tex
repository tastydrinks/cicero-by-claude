% Ad Atticum 12.20 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-20, 15 March 45 BC, Astura

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Astura on
the Ides of March 709 AUC --- 15 March 45 BC (the
manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\ Asturae Id.\
Mart.\ a.\ 709 (45)}). The shortest letter of the
cluster, written the day after 12.19. Atticus had
evidently passed on advice that Cicero ought to
\textit{dissemble} the depth of his mourning; Cicero
answers that whole days spent in reading and writing
are themselves the dissembling, even if his real
purpose is to soothe and heal his own mind, and even
if the headway he makes with himself is slight.}

\textit{The second section is the practical
counterpart to that work. He wants two pieces of
prosopographical information for the book he has
been writing on the lessening of grief (almost
certainly the lost \textit{Consolatio}): did Gnaeus
Caepio perish at sea while his father was still
living, or after his father's death? Did Rutilia
die before or after her son Gaius Cotta? The
arrangement of the question --- two near-identical
binary inquiries, drawn from the same generation
of consular families --- shows the kind of
exempla-collection that lies behind the lost
treatise.}
