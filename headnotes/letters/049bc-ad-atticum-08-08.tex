% Ad Atticum 8.8 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-08-08, 24 February 49 BC, Formian villa

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from the Formian
villa on the sixth day before the Kalends of March 49
BC (the manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\ in Formiano
vi K.\ Mart.\ a.\ 705 (49)}). Word has reached Formiae
that Domitius Ahenobarbus, holding out at Corfinium,
has surrendered himself and his men --- and that
Pompey, instead of marching to relieve him, is pushing
on for Brundisium and a sea crossing to Greece. The
letter is a single cry of disgust at that abandonment,
made up almost entirely of two anaphoric catalogues of
Pompey's failings.}

\textit{Section 1 is the indictment of Pompey, built
as a string of pluperfects --- \textit{aluerat,
coeperat, probarat, pararat, reliquerat, amiserat,
compegerat} --- one shameful verb per phase of the
disaster. Section 2 stages, against that record, the
brief mirage of a Pompey who might have turned and
fought: Cicero imagines the honourable
(\textit{to kalon}) flashing before his eyes, and puts
into his mouth a defiant tag from Sophocles' \textit{Oedipus
at Colonus} (lines 1023--4). Then he undoes the
fantasy: Pompey is in fact bidding the honourable a
long farewell and making for Brundisium. The closing
sentence breaks off: grief blocks him from writing
more.}
