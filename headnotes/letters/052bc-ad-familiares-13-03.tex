% Ad Familiares 13.3 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-03, around April 52 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero to C. Memmius, written from Rome around
April 52 BC or shortly after (the manuscripts:
\textit{Scr. Romae, ut videtur, post m. Apr. 702 aut
in. a. 703}). The addressee is the same C. Memmius
addressed in \textit{Fam.} 13.1 and 13.2, and the form
is the small letter of recommendation that runs through
the early part of Book 13. Here Cicero commends one
A. Fufius, otherwise unknown, on whose behalf Memmius
has apparently already given him a face-to-face
undertaking.}

\textit{The letter is a single sentence in Latin, a
miniature in the form. The leverage Cicero applies is
not pressure but courtesy: Memmius has already
promised, so all Cicero asks is that the promise be
honoured \textit{ut mihi coram recepisti} (``in the way
you assured me in person''). The closing turn ---
Fufius will bind himself to Memmius for life through
his \textit{officium} and \textit{observantia} ---
locates the favour in the ordinary economy of Roman
patronage: a service done now produces a client
permanently attached to the patron. The brevity is the
register; longer recommendations in the same book
(\textit{Fam.} 13.5--6, 13.13) show what Cicero produces
when the case needs argument rather than reminder.}
