% Ad Familiares 16.19 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-16-19, c. 15 August 45 BC, Tusculum

\textit{Cicero from his Tusculan villa to Tiro in Rome, a short
covering note from the summer of 45 BC. The Perseus dateline places
it ``a little after the beginning of Sextilis (August) 709''
(c.\ mid-August 45). Tiro is again away on errands.}

\textit{The letter is almost telegraphic: send Demetrius back, finish
the Aufidius business, and if that is not what is keeping you, fly
here. ``I am waiting for your letters on a number of matters --- and
for you yourself much more'' opens with the affectionate impatience
that defines the cluster; it ends, in three syllables of Latin, with
\textit{vale}.}
