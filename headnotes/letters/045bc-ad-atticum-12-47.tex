% Ad Atticum 12.47 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-47, 16 May 45 BC, Lanuvium

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Lanuvium on
the seventeenth day before the Kalends of June 709
AUC --- 16 May 45 BC (the manuscript dateline:
\textit{Scr.\ Lanuvi xvii K.\ Iun.\ a.\ 709 (45)}).
Cicero has broken the journey from Astura at his
Lanuvine villa; the letter is all business and all
about the shrine fund. Two prospective sellers are in
play: Mustela's parcel (large), and the gardens of
Clodia. The Faberian bond --- a debt instrument from
Faberius that Cicero hopes to convert into the
purchase price --- has to be cleared either way, so
Atticus is asked to sound out Balbus on Caesar's
side.}

\textit{The closing flicker of self-mockery ---
\textit{etsi de cupiditate nemini concedam}, ``in
covetousness I shall yield to no one'' --- is the
first hint of humour in the sequence, set against the
businesslike inventory of obstacles: covetous seller,
wealthy seller, heir of a complicated estate. More
face to face when Atticus next visits: the meeting
arranged at the close of \textit{ad Att.} 12.46 is
nearly upon them.}
