% Ad Atticum 12.27 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-27, 23 March 45 BC, Astura

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Astura on the
tenth day before the Kalends of April 709 AUC ---
23 March 45 BC (the manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\
Asturae x K.\ Apr.\ a.\ 709 (45)}). The search for a
\textit{horti} to house the shrine for Tullia drives
the first section. Sicca has now arrived and Cicero
expects the Silius terms today; a smaller alternative
property of Cotta's, beyond Silius', is too cramped
and lacks the \textit{celebritas} --- the frequented
public setting --- that Cicero requires for the
\textit{fanum}. If Atticus can close the Silius deal,
the Cotta option falls away.}

\textit{The second section turns to young Cicero's
allowance and to the daily letter-discipline that has
become its own ritual: ``I see from your letters, and
you doubtless see from mine, that we have nothing to
write about: the same things day after day, things
already worn to nothing. Yet I cannot help sending to
you every day, so that I may receive your letters in
return.'' The third returns to Brutus, who is
travelling to meet Pansa; Cicero, fleeing Rome ``for
many reasons,'' is already weighing whether to make
some excuse for not seeing him. The grief and the
practical projects share the same elliptical
register.}
