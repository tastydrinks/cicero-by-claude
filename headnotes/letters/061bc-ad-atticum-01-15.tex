% Ad Atticum 1.15 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-01-15, Ides (15) March 61 BC, written at Rome

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written at Rome on the Ides of
March 61 BC --- a short note carrying news that Quintus
Cicero, after his praetorship, has drawn Asia by lot as
his proconsular province for the next year. The
\textit{Iliad} tag in the second sentence
(\textsection 1, ``be mindful of every kind of virtue'')
is from Hector's last appeal to Achilles in Book 22 ---
the proper note for a brother going east into the great
wealth and the great temptations of an Asian command. The
proper letter to Quintus on how to govern (\textit{Ad
Quintum Fratrem} 1.1, the small treatise on provincial
administration) follows in 60 BC. Of his own affairs
Cicero says nothing here; the substance will go in a
letter Quintus himself will carry.}
