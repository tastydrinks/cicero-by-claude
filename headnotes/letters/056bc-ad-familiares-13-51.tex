% Ad Familiares 13.51 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-51, undated (placeholder 56 BC), written at Rome

\textit{Cicero to Publius Caesius, written at Rome at an
uncertain date --- Perseus's tradition (\textit{loco et anno
incerto}) groups it with the recommendation letters and
provisionally before 56 BC. The letter is addressed to a man
otherwise unattested in the corpus; the chief subject, Publius
Messienus, is also obscure. The shape, however, is the
standard \textit{commendatio}: an inherited friendship between
the writers' fathers as the warrant, the request that the
addressee take Messienus \textit{in fidem suam}, the closing
formula that Caesius will both gain a worthy man and oblige
Cicero by doing so.}
