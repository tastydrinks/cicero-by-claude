% Ad Familiares 11.24 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-11-24, 6 June 43 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero to D. Junius Brutus Albinus, imperator and
consul-elect, from Rome on 6 June 43 BC --- Perseus dateline
\textit{Scr. Romae viii Id. Iun. a. 711 (43)}. Written two
days after the longer 11.21, in answer to a short, telegram-
brisk dispatch from Brutus in Cisalpine Gaul. Brutus has
reported that he is well and getting better, that Lepidus
is ``leaning the right way'' (a hope that would be overtaken
within days), and that with three armies in play --- his
own, Plancus's, and Octavian's --- nothing should now be
feared.}

\textit{Cicero matches his correspondent's brevity for once
and lets the dry humour show. He has, he says, ``champed the
bit'' as Brutus told him to --- the Latin idiom for putting
up with restraint --- and is ready to hand over his
\textit{vigilia}, his nightwatch in the city, to Brutus, on
condition that doing so does not lower his own steadiness.
On the operational point: if the enemy permits, Brutus
should wait in Italy for further letters before moving (the
city has business he is wanted for); if his coming will end
the war outright, nothing is more important. The senate has
voted him the funds nearest to hand; Servius Sulpicius
Rufus is acting as his friend in town; Cicero is not
failing in his part.}
