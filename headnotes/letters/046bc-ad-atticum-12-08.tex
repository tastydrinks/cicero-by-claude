% Ad Atticum 12.8 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-08, intercalary month, 46 BC, Tusculum

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Tusculum during
the intercalary month at the end of 46 BC (the
manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\ in Tusculano m.\
interc.\ post a.\ 708 (46)}). A single short section,
continuing the conversation of 12.7 about young
Marcus's Spanish project. The first installment of his
allowance is due and ``the man is in a hurry,'' so the
practical question of timing presses. Cicero asks
Atticus for a report from Celer on Caesar's dealings
with the candidates --- and whether Caesar's own mind
is set on the \textit{fenicularium}, the
``Fennel-field'' of southern Spain where he is
preparing to fight Pompey's sons, or on the
\textit{Martium campum}, the Campus Martius in Rome,
where the consular elections would be held. The pun
on the two campuses is the whole letter's wit. A
closing note: Cicero owes a visit to Pilia and Attica
at Rome, and wants to know whether the elections
require him there anyway.}
