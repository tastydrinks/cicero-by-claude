% Ad Atticum 5.3 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-05-03, 11 May 51 BC, written at Trebula

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written at the Trebulan villa of Pontius
at first light on 11 May 51 BC, the day after Cicero had arrived
there from Pompeii (the journey forecast in Ad Atticum 5.2 of
the previous day). The letter is a one-section travel bulletin
prefaced by the report that a doubled letter has just come in
from Atticus --- on the third day from sending, a quick run ---
and that another, more important one may be in transit
unrecovered: a Greek aside (\textit{spoudaioteron}, ``more
urgent'') registers the worry.}

\textit{The rest is logistics and household: a request for the
rumour traffic at Rome --- the country towns are showing the
highest fear, says Cicero, but most of it empty ---, a promise
to put Atticus in good standing with Lentulus, an affectionate
note that Dionysius the tutor is well thought of and that
Atticus's freedman Nicanor is giving outstanding service. The
plan for the day is Beneventum, the next stop south. One
corrupt verb at the close is preserved with the obeli.}
