% Ad Atticum 12.38 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-38, 6 May 45 BC, Astura

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Astura on the
day before the Nones of May 709 AUC --- 6 May 45 BC
(the manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\ Asturae prid.\
Non.\ Mai.\ a.\ 709 (45)}). The first section is the
shortest possible note: a courier has come back
without a letter, presumably because Atticus was too
busy, and Cicero forgives the silence in advance.
The phrase \textit{nihil equidem levor sed tamen
aberro} catches the precise temperature of the Astura
days --- writing brings no relief, only a kind of
turning aside from himself.}

\textit{The second section is darker. Asinius Pollio
has written about an \textit{impurus cognatus} ---
``our impure kinsman,'' the standard reading being
Cicero's nephew Quintus the younger, whose conduct
has been a running source of family scandal. Balbus
the younger had hinted at it; Dolabella had spoken
obscurely; Pollio has spelled it out. Cicero refuses
to give the matter room for \textit{nova aegrimonia},
``fresh sorrow'' --- the Tullia wound is the only one
this spring has any space for --- and breaks off
mid-sentence (\textit{quamquam mihi quidem---}) with
the Stoic injunction \textit{tenendus dolor est},
``the pain must be held in.'' The grief vocabulary of
the cluster (\textit{dolor}, \textit{maeror}) is now
being extended to second-order sorrows the writer
will not allow himself.}
