% Ad Atticum 16.12 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-16-12, 13 November 44 BC, in Puteolano

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written at the Puteolan villa on
or about 13 November 44 BC. The Perseus dateline reads
\textit{Scr. in Puteolano vht Id. Nov. a. 710 (44)}, in which
\textit{vht} is plainly OCR-corrupt; Shackleton Bailey
places the letter at the Ides of November (13 November). A
single short paragraph, written in haste before he leaves
the coast.}

\textit{Cicero has been waiting for Atticus to write back
about Ocella; Atticus has dithered (``\textit{muginaris}'')
and sent nothing, so Cicero has made up his own mind. He
will be at Rome on the day before the Ides --- better to be
there in vain than to be needed and absent --- and he is
also afraid of being cut off if Antony moves. The letter
closes on warmer notes: praise of Varro's Heraclidean
\textit{logos} (which 16.11 mentioned he had not yet got
hold of); more to come in person.}
