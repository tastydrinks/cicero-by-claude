% Ad Atticum 12.48 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-48, 17 May 45 BC, Lanuvium

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Lanuvium on
the morning of the sixteenth day before the Kalends of
June 709 AUC --- 17 May 45 BC (the manuscript
dateline: \textit{Scr.\ Lanuvi xvi K.\ Iun.\ mane a.\
709 (45)}). A morning note before the day's last
stage of the journey. Cicero has read whatever Hirtius
has sent --- almost certainly the anti-Catonian
pamphlet, written at Cicero's prompting --- and tells
Atticus to keep circulating it. Philotimus, Terentia's
freedman and a recurring trouble in the settlement
business, gets a single agreeing line.}

\textit{The middle of the letter is the cluster's
quiet domestic register: Caesar moving in next door to
Atticus is a wry boost to property values; the
courier is expected back with news of Pilia and
Attica; Atticus is happy at home. The close is the
arrangement that drives the next two letters --- Tiro
has been told Atticus will come to the Tusculanum at
once, and Cicero will hold him to it.}
