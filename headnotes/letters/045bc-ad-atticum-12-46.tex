% Ad Atticum 12.46 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-12-46, 15 May 45 BC, Astura

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Astura on the
Ides of May 709 AUC --- 15 May 45 BC (the manuscript
dateline: \textit{Scr.\ Asturae Id.\ Mai.\ a.\ 709
(45)}). The shortest letter of the sequence and the
hinge of the northward move: Cicero resolves to leave
Astura, stop at Lanuvium, and press on to the
Tusculanum. The reasoning is bleak and exact ---
either the estate is to be abandoned for good (the
grief will follow him anywhere, only better hidden),
or there is no point waiting ten years to face it.}

\textit{The second paragraph turns the same logic on
his reading: he is half-afraid the books are making
him softer, not harder. The line that closes the
first section --- \textit{exculto enim animo nihil
agreste, nihil inhumanum est}, ``to a cultivated mind
nothing is uncouth, nothing inhuman'' --- is the kind
of philosophical maxim he has been writing his way
into all spring, and a small private answer to the
ferocity of his own grief. The brief closing arranges
the meeting: two letters will do, he will come out to
Atticus if needed.}
