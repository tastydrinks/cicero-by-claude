% Ad Atticum 2.2 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-02-02, December 60 BC, written from a country villa

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from one of the country
villas (Antium or Tusculum: ``Scr. ad villam'' is the
Perseus dateline) in December 60 BC. A short, intimate
note: the family business --- ``take care of our little
Cicero'' (the son, then four years old) --- and a literary
report. Cicero is reading Dicaearchus, the Peripatetic
historian-geographer of the third century BC: the books
\textit{On the Pellenians}, \textit{On the Corinthians},
and \textit{On the Athenians}. Atticus has provided the
first; the others Cicero already has at Rome. The closing
business is a forthcoming Antonian trial that hangs on the
proconsul's return from Macedonia, and a dinner invitation
for the day before the Kalends.}
