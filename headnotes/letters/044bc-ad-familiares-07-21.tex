% Ad Familiares 7.21 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-07-21, c. late June 44 BC, Tusculum

\textit{Cicero to C. Trebatius Testa, written from Tusculum around
the end of June 44 BC. A short businesslike note, almost wholly
shop-talk between two men who shared friends in the Roman bar.
P. Silius --- a friend whom Cicero ranks just below Trebatius
himself --- is litigating his title to the estate of one Turpilia,
and a wager (\textit{sponsio}) on whether the praetor Q. Caepio
has validly granted bonorum possessio is to settle the issue.}

\textit{Trebatius had told Cicero in conversation that the wager
could safely be entered; but Servius Sulpicius and Ofilius hold
that Turpilia's will was no will at all, since she lacked the
\textit{testamenti factio} required to make one. Silius has not
yet spoken with Trebatius directly. Cicero asks his friend, with
some emphasis, to go to Silius unprompted and undertake his case
--- and to do it at once.}
