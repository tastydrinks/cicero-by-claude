% Ad Atticum 13.3 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-13-03, 30 May 45 BC, Tusculanum

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from the
Tusculanum on the third day before the Kalends of
June 709 AUC --- 30 May 45 BC (the manuscript
dateline: \textit{Scr.\ in Tusculano iii K.\ Iun.\
a.\ 709 (45)}). A week after 13.01, and back wholly
on the business of the gardens: Atticus has reported
on the available \textit{nomina} --- the
assigned-debt claims that Cicero proposes to use as
payment instruments --- and Cicero accepts them, with
only mild reproach for Atticus's hedging. The voice
is the comfortable shop-talk of two old men who have
been doing each other's accounts for thirty years:
``if I were managing my own affair, I should manage
nothing except by your advice.'' Caelius's
\textit{nomen} is out (Atticus does not approve it);
the rest are in.}

\textit{The second section turns to the personal
business that always sits alongside the financial.
Crispus and Mustela --- the two partners holding
shares in the property --- need to be coordinated;
the share each holds is something Cicero would like
fixed. And Brutus is on his way back from Cisalpina,
the news already brought by Aegypta, the freedman
courier, whose letter Cicero is forwarding to Atticus
because it was, he says, \textit{commode scripta} ---
``conveniently'' put. The textual crux at
$\dagger$espraes$\dagger$ in section 1 (perhaps
\textit{ex praesenti}, ``out of ready money'') leaves
the precise mechanism of payment in shadow; the
direction of the negotiation, however, is plain.}
