% Ad Familiares 12.11 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-12-11, 7 March 43 BC, in camp at Tarichea

\textit{C. Cassius (proconsul) to Cicero, from the camp at Tarichea
on 7 March 43 BC --- Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. in castris
Tadolicis Non. Mart. a. 711 (43)}, with the subscription
\emph{ex castris taricheis}. A military dispatch in the formulaic
opening of campaign correspondence (\emph{si vales bene est, ego
quidem valeo}, here abbreviated). Cassius reports the consolidation
of force in the East that gives \emph{Fam.} 12.4 and 12.5 their
substance: the armies of L. Staius Murcus and Q. Marcius Crispus have
been turned over to him, together with the legion of Q. Caecilius
Bassus and the four legions A. Allienus brought out of Egypt. The
note to Cicero is brief, decisive, and meant to be read at Rome by
the Senate: a senior commander assuring the civilian leader that the
republican cause has armies in the field.}
