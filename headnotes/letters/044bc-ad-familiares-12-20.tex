% Ad Familiares 12.20 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-12-20, 2 September 44 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero to Q. Cornificius, from Rome on 2 September 44 BC ---
Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. Romae iv Non. Sept. a. 710 (44)}. The note
is short, light, and dropped between obligations: Cicero is at his
Senate bench (``this I scribbled while in the Senate'') and the great
political confrontation with Antony is hours away --- the
\textit{First Philippic} is delivered the same day. Cornificius, now back
from his African province, has evidently passed by Cicero's little
overnight lodge at Sinuessa without stopping; Cicero pretends to take
offence on the villa's behalf and demands a written ``memorandum''
\textit{[Greek: hypomnematismon]} of any visit at the more impressive
Cumanum or Pompeianum by way of restitution. The closing tease ---
``I find it easier to answer than to challenge,'' but I will challenge
if you go on idling --- is the easy chaff of a long literary friendship,
preserved here at the very moment the political voice is about to be
unsheathed.}
