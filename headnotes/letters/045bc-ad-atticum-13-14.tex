% Ad Atticum 13.14 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-13-14, 27 June 45 BC, Arpinum

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from his ancestral
estate at Arpinum on \textit{v K.\ Quint.} --- 27 June
709 AUC, 45 BC. Cicero has come up from the
Tusculanum to put the small properties in order
before Caesar's return makes travel impossible (see
13.9), and the rains, as 13.16 will report, are
keeping him indoors. The letter is two short
paragraphs: a query whether Atticus approves the
plan to dedicate the \textit{Academica} to Varro
(with Atticus himself written in as a third
speaker), and a piece of domestic anxiety because
Attica has been silent three days.}

\textit{The register is the unhurried intimacy of two old
correspondents at a slow stretch: half writing-room
business (\textit{nomina iam facta sunt}, ``the names
have been assigned,'' i.e. the dramatis personae of
the dialogue are set, but the casting can still be
changed), half the small confession that with no
messenger arrived from Atticus there has been
nothing of his own to write either. No Greek phrases
in this letter; no cruxes.}
