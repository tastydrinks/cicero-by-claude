% Ad Familiares 13.70 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-70, 46 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero at Rome to P. Servilius Isauricus, proconsul of
Asia, written in the course of 46 BC (the manuscript dateline:
\textit{Scr. Romae, ut videtur, a. 708}). A brief recommendation
of T. Ampius Menander, the freedman of T. Ampius Balbus, one of
Cicero's closest friends. The letter is short and frames its own
weight: Cicero acknowledges that many ask to be recommended to
Servilius through him, and that he sometimes obliges
indiscriminately --- but here he is writing for one of his
intimates, and that should be felt.}

\textit{The middle piece in the Servilius recommendation cluster
(13.68--72). T. Ampius Balbus, the patron, was a Pompeian who had
been pardoned by Caesar; Menander's affairs in Asia would have
needed protection from a sympathetic proconsul. The closing
double formula --- ``You will do me a very welcome service \ldots\
this I most earnestly beg of you, again and again'' --- is the
standard rhetorical sign-off of the recommendation genre.}
