% Ad Familiares 13.72 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-72, 46 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero at Rome to P. Servilius Isauricus, proconsul of
Asia, written in the course of 46 BC (the manuscript dateline:
\textit{Scr. Romae, ut videtur, a. 708}), probably in the spring
or early summer, when Servilius was newly out in his province.
The salutation hails Servilius as \textit{conlega} --- ``my
colleague'' --- the formal Roman address-title that marks ex-
consular peerage: Servilius had been consul with Caesar in 48 BC,
Cicero himself in 63 BC, and the formula is the one register
reserved for men who had stood in that highest of magistracies.}

\textit{The letter is a follow-up to a recommendation Cicero had
made in person, in Servilius's gardens at Rome before the
proconsul's departure, on behalf of Caerellia --- a wealthy and
cultivated Roman woman, a close friend of Cicero, who held debts,
business interests, and landed property in Asia. Cicero's tone is
the easy one of an old colleague calling in a promise already
given. The technical lever he points to is a \textit{senatus
consultum} concerning the heirs of one C. Vennonius, evidently
some recent ruling that bore on Asian creditors; Cicero, with the
deference proper to the cluster's politics, leaves the
interpretation of the decree to Servilius's own \textit{sapientia}
and to his standing respect for the Senate's authority. The
Perseus dateline is year-precision; \texttt{meta/works.yaml} may

\texttt{046bc-}.}
