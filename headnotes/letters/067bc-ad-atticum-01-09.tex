% Ad Atticum 1.9 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-01-09, March or April 67 BC

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written at Rome in March or
April 67 BC. A note explicitly short because Cicero does
not know where Atticus is and refuses to entrust intimate
talk to a letter that may go astray --- the first instance
of a recurring anxiety that will mark the corpus through
the civil wars. The body of the letter is once again the
art-shipment business: the Megarian statues, the Hermae,
``what is most in the gymnasium style.'' At the end the
poet Thyillus (one of Atticus's Greek h\'erangers) asks
Cicero to ask Atticus for a copy of the \textit{Eumolpid\=on
patria}, the unwritten laws of the Eleusinian priests, and
Cicero passes the request along.}
