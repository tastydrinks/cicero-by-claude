% Ad Atticum 1.7 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-01-07, just before 13 February 67 BC

\textit{Cicero to Atticus at Athens, written at Rome shortly
before the Ides of February 67 BC. A short note: a payment
to Cincius (Atticus's freedman and Roman agent), a request
that the items Atticus has bought reach Rome quickly, and
the first surviving mention of the library Atticus is to
help build for him. The closing sentence, ``all the hope of
our pleasures, when we come into our leisure, I have placed
in your kindness,'' is the future Cicero of the philosophical
works already showing his hand.}
