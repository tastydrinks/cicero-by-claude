% Ad Familiares 11.23 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-11-23, 25 May 43 BC, Eporedia

\textit{D. Brutus to Cicero, from camp at Eporedia on 25 May 43
BC --- Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. Eporediae viii K. Iun. a.
711 (43)}. A short field-dispatch, written from the top of
the Po valley as Brutus's army moves north-west to make
contact with Plancus. The note is calibrated to steady
Cicero: the army is well, Lepidus is showing the right
disposition (Brutus has not yet heard of the defection of
30 May), and between Brutus, Plancus, and Lepidus the
Republic has three armies of its own in the field.}

\textit{The closing image is from horsemanship: ``if you
take the bit between your teeth'' (\textit{si frenum
momorderis}) is the rider's command to a horse to charge ---
let Cicero only press the attack in the Senate, and the
optimates' detractors will not stand against him.}
