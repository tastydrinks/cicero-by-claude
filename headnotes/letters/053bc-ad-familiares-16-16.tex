% Ad Familiares 16.16 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-16-16, late May 53 BC, Transalpine Gaul

\textit{Quintus Cicero to his brother Marcus, written from
Transalpine Gaul --- where Quintus was serving as a legate
under Caesar --- at the end of May 53 BC. The letter is one
of the very few in the Ciceronian corpus by a hand other than
Marcus's, and it preserves Quintus's voice clearly: warmer
and less guarded than his brother's, given to exclamations
and oaths, and grateful in a way that does not stop to weigh
its phrases. The occasion is Tiro's manumission, which Marcus
has just carried out and reported in two letters --- one in
his own hand, one in Tiro's --- both of which Quintus has
read straight through.}

\textit{The first section opens with the family oath formula
\textit{ita te \dots\ videam, ut} --- ``so may I look upon you,
and my Cicero, and my Tulliola, and your son'' --- one of the
strongest in the household register, calling the children of
the family as witnesses. Quintus's reflex is comparative: his
own loyal slave Statius is already a great pleasure to him,
and yet Tiro brings on top of that gift the literary
collaboration and the \textit{humanitas} that Statius does
not. The closing line about promising things to Sabinus's
boys is one of the small puzzles of book 16: most likely the
slave-couriers who carried Marcus's news up into Gaul, whom
Quintus has just paid off and means to reward further.}
