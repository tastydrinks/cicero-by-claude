% Ad Atticum 3.19 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-03-19, 16 September 58 BC, written at Thessalonica

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from Thessalonica on the
sixteenth day before the Kalends of October (16 September) 58
BC. The pivotal logistical letter of the Thessalonican stretch:
Cicero has decided that, with the action of 58 BC effectively
closed, the Asian plan must be set aside (``crowds are a thing
I hate''), and that the new magistrates' action --- if any ---
must find him not too far away. The destination is Epirus, in
Atticus's own estate. \textsection 2 names the people whose
prayers and promises hold him to life: Quintus, Sestius and
the others at Rome, Terentia, Tullia, and Atticus himself.
\textsection 3 is the brief, controlled cri de c\oe ur ---
``assign me from your land only as much as my body can take
up.''}
