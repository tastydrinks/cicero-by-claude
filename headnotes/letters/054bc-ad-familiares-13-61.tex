% Ad Familiares 13.61 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-61, undated (placeholder 54 BC), Cilicia / Bithynia province

\textit{Cicero to Publius Silius, propraetor of Bithynia and
Pontus, undated --- Perseus's tradition places it about the
same time as \textit{Fam.} 13.53, during Cicero's
proconsulship of Cilicia in 51--50 BC. Silius is the
addressee of a small cluster of letters from this season
(\textit{Fam.} 13.61--65), all on the same model of
recommendation.}

\textit{The matter is the estate of T. Pinnius. Pinnius the
elder had named Cicero \textit{tutor} to his son and
secondary heir, a mark of intimate trust; his son, still a
boy of cultivated parts, is to receive eight million
sesterces from the city of Nicaea, the chief city of
Bithynia, which apparently owes the estate that sum and is
willing to pay. The single ask is that Silius use his
governor's authority to make sure as much of it is paid as
\textit{fides} and \textit{dignitas} allow. The letter is
the shortest of the Silius cluster: a guardian's brief
intervention on behalf of his ward.}
