% Ad Familiares 14.24 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-14-24, 11 August 47 BC, Brundisium

\textit{Cicero to Terentia, written from Brundisium on the
third day before the Ides of Sextilis 47 BC --- 11 August,
by the Perseus dateline (\textit{Scr. Brundisi iii Id.
Sext. a. 707 (47)}). It is the eighth month of the
Brundisium captivity. Caesar is in the East, the
Republican remnant is still in arms in Africa, Cicero
has no political cover, and the only news that matters
is when Caesar will come back across the Adriatic and
what reception he will give the proconsul who turned for
home at the harbour at Patrae.}

\textit{The letter is four lines long. He has nothing
certain to report --- not about Caesar's arrival, not
about the dispatch that Philotimus (Terentia's freedman
and household agent) is rumoured to be carrying. When
something is certain, he will send word at once. Take
care of your health. The pair with 14.23, written the
next day, registers the moment the dispatch arrives.}
