\textit{Cicero to Q. Cornificius (governor of Africa Vetus), from Rome at an uncertain month of 44 BC ---
Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. Romae mense incerto a. 710 (44)}. A short commendation on behalf of C. Anicius, a friend of Cicero's traveling to Africa on a \textit{legatio libera} --- the senatorial perquisite that allowed a senator to take a fictive embassy abroad in order to transact private business under the dignity (and lictors) of an official mission. Cicero asks Cornificius for the customary courtesies and presses one in particular: the assignment of lictors to senatorial legates, which Cicero says he himself granted in his own province without being asked, on the precedent of ``the foremost men.''}
