% Ad Atticum 9.7 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-09-07, 13 March 49 BC, Formian villa

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written from the Formian
villa on the third day before the Ides of March 49 BC
(the manuscript dateline: \textit{Scr.\ in Formiano iii
Id.\ Mart.\ a.\ 705 (49)}). Brundisium has not yet
fallen; Pompey is still on the Italian coast, Caesar is
moving down the peninsula, and Cicero --- still at
Formiae, still not embarked --- has just received a
full and consoling answer from Atticus to the long
deliberative letter of a few days before. The reply
arrived by the swift-footed courier whom Salvius had
promised; it gave Cicero, in his own image, a little
\textit{animulae instillatum}, a few drops of life
trickled in. The letter encloses copies of correspondence
with the Caesarian intermediaries Balbus and Oppius and
a letter of Caesar's to them, sent on so that Atticus
may judge the temper of the other camp.}

\textit{The letter is heavily marked by Greek tags ---
the one thing essential, \textit{[Greek: to syn\'echon]};
the meeting, \textit{[Greek: ap\'ant\=esis]}; the
political role of peace-broker, \textit{[Greek:
pol\'iteuma]}; the silent reservation \textit{[Greek:
m\'e moi]} that Pompey may turn against him; ``I speak
from knowledge,'' \textit{[Greek: eid\'os soi l\'eg\=o]};
the charge of ingratitude, \textit{[Greek:
acharist\'ias]}; the sailing season, \textit{[Greek:
pl\'oos h\=ora\^ios]}. Section 3 contains the famous
formulation: \textit{beneficium sequor, non causam}
--- ``I am following the obligation, not the cause'' ---
the same calculus as in his defence of Milo. Section 4
is the bleak diagnosis of Pompey's strategy: starve
Italy, ravage the fields, plunder the rich. Section 5
turns to the wished-for delay, in which the sailing
season may steal up on the indecision; if Pompey's
health holds, Cicero believes, Caesar will not be left
a roof-tile in Italy. The letter closes on a small,
domestic note: Atticus must walk and take his rubdown,
Cicero must sleep --- the long letter has brought him
sleep at last.}
