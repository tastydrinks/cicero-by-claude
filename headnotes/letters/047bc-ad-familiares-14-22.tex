% Ad Familiares 14.22 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-14-22, 31 August 47 BC, Brundisium

\textit{Cicero to Terentia, written from Brundisium on the
day before the Kalends of September 47 BC --- 31 August,
by the Perseus dateline (\textit{Scr. Brundis i K. Sept.
a. 707 (47)}); the subscription line on the letter itself
reads \textit{K. Septemb.}, the morning the courier left.
Three weeks on from the cautious good news of 14.23
(``a letter from Caesar has at last reached me, and it
is generous enough''), and Cicero is back to waiting.
The same moment is registered in \textit{Att.} 11.22:
the Pharnaces--Caesar news from Asia, Caesar's eastward
detour, the captivity dragging on through another
month.}

\textit{The note is three lines. Every day they are
expecting their own couriers; if the couriers come,
perhaps there will be something definite to act on, and
he will pass it on at once. Take careful care of your
health. Farewell. Caesar will not in fact reach Italy
until well into September, and the meeting at Tarentum
is still weeks away.}
