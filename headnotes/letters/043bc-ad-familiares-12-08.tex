\textit{Cicero to C. Cassius, from Rome around 9 June 43 BC ---
Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. Romae v Id. Iun. aut paulo post a. 711 (43)}.
A short, electric note written just after the news of Lepidus's defection to
Antonius had reached Rome --- the moment when the victory at Mutina was
revealed to be no victory at all. Cicero gives the political reading in a
single breath: the war they had thought finished is renewed, and the
constitutional cause now rests on four men --- D. Brutus and Plancus for the
immediate present, Cassius and M. Brutus for the longer guarantee of
\emph{libertas}. The closing lines to Cassius are pure exhortation: ``set
this before you, and strive toward the highest.''}
