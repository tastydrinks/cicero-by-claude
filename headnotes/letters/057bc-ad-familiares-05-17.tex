% Ad Familiares 5.17 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-05-17, late 57 BC, written at Rome

\textit{Cicero to Publius Sittius, written at Rome in late 57
BC. Sittius, a Roman knight from Nuceria, had gone to North
Africa during the late 60s on a complicated trading and
political mission and had been suspected of involvement with
the Catilinarian conspiracy --- whence the line
``\textsection 2: in those first times, in which you, absent,
were being called into hatred and into accusation, I
defended you.'' He had stayed in Mauretania and Numidia,
serving the Mauretanian king Bocchus and building a private
condottiere force; he would die in 44 BC, having joined
Caesar at Thapsus. Cicero defended his son Publius after his
own return from exile in autumn 57 BC, on a charge that did
not stand. The letter is the formal consolatio after that
trial: a declaration that Sittius's courage is in his son,
and that the wise are moved by shame and not by chance.}
