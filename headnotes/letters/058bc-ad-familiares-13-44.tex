% Ad Familiares 13.44 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-13-44, before 58 BC (some months after Fam. 13.43), written at Rome

\textit{Cicero to M. Fadius Gallus (the same correspondent of
the better-known \textit{Fam.} 7.23 on the bust-buying), some
months after the preceding letter (\textit{Fam.} 13.43) and
before Cicero's exile of 58 BC. A two-sentence letter of
commendation: he reminds Gallus of his earlier recommendation
of L. Oppius and L. Egnatius, and asks him to do for them
all that intimate friendship requires. Of literary interest
mostly as one of a long stream of recommendation letters
which dominate \textit{Ad Familiares} book 13 and which
together constitute a working manual for a Roman senator's
\textit{officium} of patronage.}
