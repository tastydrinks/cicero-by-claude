% Ad Familiares 5.5 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-05-05, late November / early December 62 BC

\textit{Cicero to Gaius Antonius (the consul of 63 BC who
had been Cicero's colleague and was now proconsul of
Macedonia), written at Rome at the end of November or
beginning of December 62 BC. The cool, formal opening
acknowledges that the friendship has cracked: Cicero would
have written nothing but commendations were Antonius not a
former colleague, but Atticus is going east and forces a
real letter. The middle paragraph is the bill of grievance
--- Antonius has, on Cicero's account, ignored or even
spoken against him --- with the famous gentle aside, ``I
do not dare to say that I have `ascertained' it, lest I
happen to use that very word which men say is wont to be
falsely turned against me by you'' --- the verb
\textit{comperisse}, ``to have ascertained,'' had been the
hostile tag for Cicero's evidence-gathering against the
Catilinarian conspirators. The closing paragraph commends
Atticus and his Epirote business affairs to Antonius's
care --- the practical reason for the letter that is
otherwise mostly an ultimatum.}
