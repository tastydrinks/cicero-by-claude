\textit{D. Brutus to Cicero, from camp on the march to
Cularo on 3 June 43 BC --- Perseus dateline \textit{Scr.
in castris in itinere Cularonem iii Non. Iun. a. 711 (43)}.
A very short dispatch, written in transit toward Cularo
(modern Grenoble) as Brutus pushes north-west to link up
with Plancus. By now the news of Lepidus's defection on
30~May has reached him, and the field-dispatch register
has gone hard: brought-up legions, money or its decree,
good faith and speed in the messengers --- everything
quartermasterly, nothing rhetorical. The \textit{ista quae
acciderunt} that he had foreseen is Lepidus.}
