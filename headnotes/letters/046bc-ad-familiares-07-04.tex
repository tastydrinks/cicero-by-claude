% Ad Familiares 7.4 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-07-04, September 46 BC (intercalary month, after a. d. xiv K.), Cumae

\textit{Cicero to M. Marius, at his villa near Pompeii, written
from the Cumaean estate. The Perseus dateline gives \textit{in
Cumano m. intercal. post a. 708 (46)} --- the second intercalary
month inserted into Caesar's calendar reform of 46 ---
indicating a date in the autumn. The salutation is to M. Mario,
not to C. Trebatius Testa (the principal correspondent of Book
7); the launch metadata's recipient line was therefore
inaccurate.}

\textit{The note is a one-paragraph travel announcement to a
neighbour. Cicero has just arrived at Cumae with Scribonius Libo
and is moving on to his other Campanian villa near Pompeii in a
day or two. The closing joke about Marius's gout --- ``if you
have any engagement set with your gout, see to it that you put
it off for another day'' --- is the characteristic register of
the Marius correspondence (cf. \textit{Fam.} 7.1), in which
Cicero treats Marius's chronic complaint as a fellow guest with
its own diary, to be politely asked to step aside when better
company is in town.}
