% Ad Atticum 13.48 --- headnote
% ad-atticum-13-48, 5 August 45 BC, Tusculum

\textit{Cicero to Atticus, written at the Tusculan villa around the
Nones of Sextilis 45 BC --- Perseus dateline \textit{Scr. in
Tusculano (iii) Non. Sext. a. 709 (45)}. Two short sections of
domestic and editorial housekeeping. Lepta is asking Cicero to
come at need: a certain Babullius has died, leaving an inheritance
in which Caesar takes one twelfth and Lepta a third, and Lepta is
fretting --- irrationally, Cicero notes in Greek
(\textit{[Greek: alogos]}) --- that he will not be permitted to
keep his share. Cicero will go if summoned, not before.}

\textit{The second section turns to one of the running editorial
threads of the summer: Cicero's eulogy (\textit{laudatio}) of
Porcia, the half-sister of Cato. He has corrected the piece in
haste and is sending it to Atticus so that, if a copy ends up
with Domitius the younger or with Brutus, the corrected text is
the one circulated. The closing flick --- ``some passages I can
scarcely believe I have read'' --- captures the editor's wince
at his own colleagues' eulogies, here the rival pieces by Varro
and a certain Ollius which Cicero wants resent for a second
look.}
