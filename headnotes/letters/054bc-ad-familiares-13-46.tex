\textit{Cicero to Appuleius, the companion piece to \textit{Fam.}
13.45 and probably written shortly after. L. Nostius Zoilus is a
freedman who has been left a share in his patron's estate ---
Cicero is the coheir, which makes the two of them informal partners
in the inheritance. The letter is one of the shortest in the corpus
and the formula is unusually warm: \textit{commendo tibi sic ut unum
ex nostra domo} --- ``I commend him to you as one of my own
household.''}
