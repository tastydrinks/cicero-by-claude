% Ad Familiares 14.11 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-14-11, 15 June 47 BC, Brundisium

\textit{Cicero to his wife Terentia, written from
Brundisium on the seventeenth day before the Kalends
of Quintilis 47 BC --- 15 June (the manuscript
dateline: \textit{Scr.\ Brundisi xvii K.\ Quint.\ a.\
707 (47)}). Cicero has been pinned in Brundisium since
his return from Pompey's defeat at Pharsalus, waiting
on Caesar, estranged from Antony's authority in Italy,
and increasingly preoccupied with the slow ruin of
Tullia's marriage to Dolabella. Three days before this
letter Tullia herself arrived in Brundisium; the same
event opens Att.\ 11.17 to Atticus.}

\textit{The letter is one section and almost entirely
formula --- the greeting \textit{si vales, bene est;
valeo}, an injunction to mind her health, a date --- but
the middle carries the same charge as the matching
report to Atticus. Tullia's courage and humanity have
only sharpened his grief that, through ``our''
negligence (the wife-version of the \textit{summa
culpa mea} he writes to Atticus), her circumstances
fall so far short of what her character and standing
deserved. He has it in mind to send young Cicero to
Caesar, with Gnaeus Sallustius for company; if the
boy actually sets out, Terentia will hear of it.}
