% Ad Familiares 16.25 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-16-25, c. 15 Sept 44 BC, Athens

\textit{Marcus Cicero the younger from Athens to Tiro, mid-to-late
September 44 BC, per the Perseus dateline \textit{inter med. m.
Sept. et Oct. a.~710 (44)}. The sender is the great Cicero's son,
then in his early twenties and studying philosophy at Athens
under Cratippus; the salutation \textit{CICERO F. TIRONI SVO S.}
identifies him as the son (\textit{filius}). The note is a brief,
slightly chiding reply to a letter from Tiro that had begged off
for lack of time.}

\textit{The young Cicero's voice here is studious-anxious and a
little performatively grown-up --- the same register that runs
through his longer Athens letter (\textit{Fam.} 16.21). News of
the political crisis at home reaches him by rumour and by
messenger, and his father writes faithfully about \textit{his own
goodwill toward him}, but it is Tiro's letters about anything at
all that he most wants.}
