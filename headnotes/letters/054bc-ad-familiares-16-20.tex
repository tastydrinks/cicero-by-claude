% Ad Familiares 16.20 --- headnote
% ad-familiares-16-20, May 47 BC, Rome

\textit{Cicero at Rome to Tiro, his freedman and indispensable
literary secretary, who is convalescing under the care of the Greek
physician Metrodorus. The Perseus dateline groups the letter with
\textit{Fam.} 16.18 (15 May 47 BC); the Kalends mentioned at the
close are the Kalends of June, on which gladiatorial games were being
held. The note is short, anxious, and characteristically practical:
Cicero is genuinely worried about Tiro's health, but he also wants
the library at home put in order, with the cataloguing left to
Metrodorus's timing.}

\textit{The voice here is the private Cicero of the letters to Tiro
--- terse, affectionate, peremptory and tender by turns. Diet is the
greengrocer's department, the doctor sets the rules, and Tiro is
allowed his afternoon at the games on the Kalends provided he comes
straight back the next day. ``Take care of yourself diligently, if
you love me'' is the unembarrassed close.}
