% Pro Q. Roscio Comoedo --- headnote
% pro-roscio-comoedo, c. 76 BC (date disputed, possibly anywhere 76-66 BC)

\textit{Date disputed --- somewhere between c. 76 and 66 BC --- and the
opening of the speech is lost. A civil case before the iudex C. Calpurnius
Piso over a partnership dispute. Cicero defends the famous comic actor
Quintus Roscius (no relation to Sextus Roscius of Ameria) against the
plaintiff Gaius Fannius Chaerea, who sought fifty thousand sesterces on a
claim arising from a joint ownership in the slave-actor Panurgus, whom
Roscius had trained and Flavius of Tarquinii had killed. The speech is
narrowly technical --- on Roman partnership law, account-books, formulae,
and the difference between iudicium and arbitrium --- but is also Cicero's
warmest tribute to a friend, calling Roscius "more worthy of the senate
house by his self-restraint than of the stage by his art." It is incomplete
at the close.}
