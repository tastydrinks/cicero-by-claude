% In C. Verrem Actio Secunda II --- headnote
% in-verrem-2-2, autumn 70 BC

\textit{Book II of the five-book Actio Secunda --- ``de iurisdictione,''
on Verres's administration of justice in Sicily. The book is built out
of the praetor's set-piece miscarriages: the rigged inheritance suits
against Heraclius of Syracuse and Epicrates of Bidis (whose gymnasiarch
trainer-favourites of Verres are made to claim the estates); the
trial of Sopater of Halicyae --- bribed for acquittal, then bribed
again by the prosecutor for condemnation, and the money pocketed
both ways; and at length the Sthenius affair, the longest single
narrative in the Verrines so far, in which Verres turns on his own
host at Thermae for refusing to help him strip Scipio's bronze
ornaments of Himera --- contrives a charge of forging public records,
condemns him in absentia in his prosecutor's absence, and pursues
him to Rome until ten tribunes of the plebs in joint edict (with the
young Cicero pleading the case) refuse to hold him barred. The book
then enlarges its scale: the auctioning of provincial senatorships,
priesthoods, magistracies; the Cephaloedian intercalary month
shortened by forty-five days to keep one rival out of an election;
the censorship sold city by city from Timarchides's bedroom; the
two million sesterces extorted from the cities under the name of
``statues''; and finally the scriptura partnership's books at
Syracuse, where Cicero finds Verres's name half-erased and a fake
``Verrucius'' written in over the top --- ``the Verrine tail, as if
dipped in mud, sunk into an erasure.'' The praetor stripped
Sicilian justice down to the price of a bribe, and the book is the
record of the audit.}
