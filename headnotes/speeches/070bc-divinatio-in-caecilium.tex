% Divinatio in Caecilium --- headnote
% divinatio-in-caecilium, January 70 BC

\textit{Delivered at Rome in early 70 BC, the divinatio is a preliminary
hearing to determine which of two competing prosecutors should be
permitted to bring the extortion case against Gaius Verres for his
governorship of Sicily (73-71 BC). Cicero's rival was Quintus Caecilius
Niger, who had been Verres's quaestor and was suspected of being a
collusive prosecutor put up to throw the trial. The speech is the first
in the Verrines: a sustained argument that the Sicilians' choice of
prosecutor should be honoured, that a former quaestor of the defendant
is structurally unfit to prosecute him, and that Caecilius lacks the
training, experience, and incorruptibility the case requires. Cicero
won the divinatio and proceeded to the actio prima against Verres in
August.}
