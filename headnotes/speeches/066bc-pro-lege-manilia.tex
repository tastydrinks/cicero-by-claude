% Pro Lege Manilia --- headnote
% pro-lege-manilia, 66 BC

\textit{Cicero's first political speech, delivered as praetor in
66 BC from the Rostra in support of the law of the tribune
C. Manilius which transferred from the consul Glabrio to Pompeius
the command against Mithridates and Tigranes. The speech opens by
remarking that this is Cicero's first appearance before the people
from the Rostra: a marker, in his own life, of the moment a
forensic advocate steps into political authority. The argument
falls into three parts --- the kind of war (Mithridatic, vast,
unfinished, threatening the revenues of Asia and the credit at
Rome), the size of the war (after Lucullus's recall the king has
recovered, joined Tigranes, drawn whole peoples to him), and the
choice of general. The third part is by far the longest: a long
encomium of Pompey's four qualities --- knowledge of war, virtue,
authority, fortune --- with the closing argument turning on the
contrast between his self-restraint (the legions whose footprint
harmed no man at peace, the cities of Greek statues he reckoned
not even worth seeing) and the lust and plunder of the rest of
Rome's commanders. The speech proves successful: the law passes;
Pompey takes the command; the Mithridatic war ends with the king's
death four years later.}
