% Pro M. Fonteio --- headnote
% pro-fonteio, 69 BC

\textit{Defence in 69 BC of Marcus Fonteius, former governor
of Transalpine Gaul (74-72 BC), prosecuted for extortion. The
manuscript is mutilated --- the speech survives only in
fragments, with breaks in the opening and middle. The
prosecutor was Marcus Plaetorius; the chief witnesses against
Fonteius were the Gallic chieftains he had governed, led by
Indutiomarus of the Allobroges. Cicero's defence is built on
two arguments: that the records of Roman businessmen working
in Gaul (always present, the speech argues, wherever a Gallic
coin moves) show no trace of the embezzlements alleged; and
that the testimony of barbarian Gauls --- a people who, in
Cicero's framing, sacrifice human victims, who once besieged
the Capitoline and despoiled Delphi --- ought not to weigh
against the Roman colonists of Narbo, the allies of Massilia,
and the Roman knights and businessmen who praise Fonteius.
The closing peroration calls his mother and his sister, the
Vestal Fonteia, to embrace him in court and asks that the
prayers of a Vestal weigh more with the judges than the threats
of the Gauls. Outcome unknown.}
