\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} I had supposed that Sp. Mummius was among the ten legates --- but evidently (it does indeed stand to reason \textit{[Greek: eulogon]}) it was his brother. For he was the one at Corinth. I have sent you the \textit{Torquatus}. Do speak with Silius, as you write, and press him. He was denying that that day fell in May; this one he was not denying. But you, as in everything, will handle this too with care. About Crispus and Mustela, of course, when you have got something done.

\ciceroSection{2} Since you promise to be with us by the time Brutus arrives, that is enough --- especially as these days of yours are being consumed on a great business of mine.
