\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} O my marvellous Dolabella! For now I do call him mine; before, believe me, I had my doubts. The thing bears a great reconsideration \textit{[Greek: anathe\=or\=esin]} --- to throw men from the rock, to put them on the cross, to tear down the column, to put the place itself out to contract for paving! What more would you have? Heroic. He seems to me to have done away with the play-acting of grief which was creeping on by the day, and which, once settled in, I feared would prove dangerous to our tyrannicides.

\ciceroSection{2} Now I quite agree with what your letter says, and I hope for better things --- although I cannot bear those people who, while pretending to want peace, defend criminal acts. But they cannot have everything at once. The thing is beginning to go better than I had thought. I shall certainly not leave unless you think I can honourably do so. To my own Brutus I shall fail at no point; and this, even if I had no tie with him, I should do for the sake of his singular, his incredible courage.

\ciceroSection{3} I am handing over our Pilia's villa, and all that is in the villa, to her, on my own setting out for Pompeii on the Kalends of May. How I wish you would persuade Brutus to be at Astura!
