\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} I had just dispatched Demeas to you when Eros came to me. But in his letter there was nothing new except that the auction is two days off. From that day on, then, as you write, I should like the Faberian business wrapped up too; Eros says it cannot be today, but tomorrow morning he thinks. The man must be courted by you --- though such flatteries \textit{[Greek: kolakeiai]} are not far short of a crime. You yourself, I hope, the day after tomorrow.

\ciceroSection{2} My dear fellow, if from anywhere you can dig it out, find me who served as the ten commissioners to Mummius. Polybius does not name them. I remember Albinus the consular and Spurius Mummius; I think I have heard from Hortensius the name Tuditanus. But in Libo's annals Tuditanus is made praetor fourteen years after Mummius's consulship. That does not square at all. I want someone at Olympia or wherever it seemed good --- a political gathering \textit{[Greek: politikon syllogon]} in the manner of your friend Dicaearchus.
