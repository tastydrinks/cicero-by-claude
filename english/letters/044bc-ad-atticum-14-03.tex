\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} Your letter, at any rate, is tranquil. If only it stayed so! For Matius said it could not. And then --- our builders had gone out for grain, and came back empty-handed bringing a great rumour: that at Rome all the grain is being carted to Antony's house. A piece of panic, surely --- a Pan-fright \textit{[Greek: panikon]} --- for otherwise you would have written. Of Balbus's Corumbus, still no sign: I know the name; he is said to be a fine architect.

\ciceroSection{2} You seem to have been called in as a witness to the sealing not without reason. For they want us to think so, and I do not see why they should not feel it in their hearts as well. But what is this to us? Still, sound out Antony's frame of mind \textit{[Greek: diathesin]}; though I think he is more concerned with the calculation of his dinner parties than plotting any real mischief. If you have anything substantive to report \textit{[Greek: pragmatikon]}, write back; if not, write up the public's mood and the actors' jokes --- the cheers \textit{[Greek: episēmasian]} of the people and what the mimes have to say. My greetings to Pilia and Attica.
