\ciceroLetterOpener{D. BRVTVS S. D. M. CICERONI}

\ciceroSection{1} In the midst of my deepest distress I take what comfort I can in this: men now see I was not without reason in fearing what has come to pass. Let them deliberate whether or not to bring the legions across from Africa and from Sardinia, whether or not to send for Brutus, and whether they will grant me pay or only decree it. I have sent letters to the Senate. Believe me, unless every one of these things is done in just the way I write, we are all heading into very great peril. Look, I beg you, to the kind of men you put in charge of bringing the legions to me. Both good faith and speed are needed. 3 June, from the camp.
