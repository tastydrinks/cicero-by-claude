\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} At last, a letter-carrier from Cicero, and by Hercules a letter \textit{elegantly} \textit{[Greek: pepinomenos]} composed --- which fact in itself signifies some \textit{progress} \textit{[Greek: prokope]}; and the others write splendid things of him as well. Leonides, however, still holds to his old line; Herodes is full of the highest praise. What more? In this matter I am quite content even to be deceived, and gladly give myself out as credulous. Please let me know if anything has been written to you by Statius which bears on me.
