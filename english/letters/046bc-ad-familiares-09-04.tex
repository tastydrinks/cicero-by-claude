\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO VARRONI}

\ciceroSection{1} On possibles \textit{[Greek: peri dynatōn]} you should know that I judge along the lines of Diodorus \textit{[Greek: kata Diodōron]}. So: if you are going to come, you should know that it is necessary that you come; and if you are not, then your coming is of the class of impossibles \textit{[Greek: adynatōn]}. Now consider which judgment \textit{[Greek: krisis]} pleases you more --- that of Chrysippus, or this one, which our friend Diodotus could never quite digest. But on these things too we shall talk when we have leisure for them; this is also, according to Chrysippus, possible \textit{[Greek: kata Chrysippon dynaton]}. About Cocceius I am much obliged --- I had given that very business in charge to Atticus as well. If you do not come to us, we shall hurry to you; if you have a garden along with your library, nothing will be wanting.
