\ciceroLetterOpener{M. LEPIDVS IMP. ITER. PONT. MAX. S. D. PR. TR. PL. SENATVI POPVLO PLEBIQVE ROMANAE}

\ciceroSection{1} If you and your children are well, it is well; I and my army are in good health. I call gods and men to witness, senators, with what mind and with what spirit I have always stood toward the commonwealth, and how I have held nothing of greater moment than our common safety and liberty --- and I should have given you swift proof of this, had not fortune wrung from my hands a policy of my own. For my whole army, an uprising having broken out, kept up its accustomed practice in preserving fellow-citizens and the common peace, and compelled me --- to speak the truth --- to take up the cause of the safety and survival of so great a multitude of Roman citizens.

\ciceroSection{2} In this matter I beg and beseech you, senators, to set aside private grievances and to take counsel for the supreme welfare of the commonwealth, and not to count our pity --- and that of our army --- in a civil disagreement as a crime. If you take account of the safety and dignity of all alike, you will take better counsel for yourselves and for the commonwealth. Given on the third day before the Kalends of June, from Pons Argenteus.
