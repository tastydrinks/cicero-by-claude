\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} I received two letters from you yesterday. From the first I learned about the theatre and about Publilius --- good signs of a public mind in agreement with itself. The applause given to Lucius Cassius struck me, in fact, as a neat touch.

\ciceroSection{2} The second letter was about Madarus, in whom there is no \textit{[Greek: phalakōma]}\dag, as you suppose. He made progress, but did not last long. I was held by his talk.\dag Now, what I had written to you obscurely, perhaps, is this. He was saying that Caesar, on the occasion when I came to him at Sestius's request and was sitting waiting, remarked, ``Am I now to be so foolish as to think that even this easy-going man is my friend, when he sits so long waiting upon my convenience?'' There you have it --- a bald-pate \textit{[Greek: phalakrōma]} that is the bitterest enemy of leisure: I mean Brutus.

\ciceroSection{4} I was thinking: to Tusculum today, Lanuvium tomorrow, then Astura. Pilia's lodging is ready; but I should have liked Attica's too. Still, I forgive you. My greetings to them both.
