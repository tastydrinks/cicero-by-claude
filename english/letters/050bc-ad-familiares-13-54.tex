\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO THERMO PROPR. S.}

Many things you have done at my recommendation are pleasing to me, but in the first place that you have treated M. Marcilius, the son of my friend and interpreter, in the most generous fashion; for he has come to Laodicea and given the warmest thanks to you in my presence, and to me on your account. Wherefore, for what remains, I ask this of you: since you are bestowing a service on grateful men, that you oblige them the more willingly, and take pains, so far as your honour permits, that the young man's mother-in-law be not made defendant. I was before this commending Marcilius in earnest, and now I commend him much more earnestly, because in his long term of attendance I have come to know the singular and almost incredible loyalty, restraint, and modesty of his father Marcilius.
