\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} The letter I sent to Caesar --- it slipped my mind to send you the copy at the time. It was not what you suspect, that I was ashamed before you of cutting a ridiculous figure as \dag micillus \dag, nor, by Hercules, did I write it any differently than if I had been writing to my peer and equal \textit{[Greek: pros ison homoion]}. For I think well of those books, as I have said to you face to face. So I wrote without flattery \textit{[Greek: akolakeutos]}, and yet in such a way that I expect there is nothing he will read with more pleasure.

\ciceroSection{2} About Attica I now at last have certainty; so congratulate her on my behalf afresh. The whole of Tigellius, and as soon as may be; for I am hanging in suspense. I tell you, Quintus tomorrow; but whether to me or to you I do not know. He wrote me he would be in Rome on the eighth before the Kalends, but I have sent someone to invite him out. Though by Hercules I shall have to come up to Rome myself, or he will be flying over before I get there.
