\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} Two letters in one day from Octavian, now indeed urging that I come to Rome at once: he wants to handle the matter through the Senate. To which I say that the Senate cannot meet before the Kalends of January --- as in fact I believe. But he adds, ``on your counsel.'' To make it short: he presses, I \textit{[Greek: skēptomai]} --- I plead an excuse. I have no confidence in his youth, I do not know in what spirit he is acting. I want nothing done without your Pansa. I fear that Antony may prove too strong, and I am loath to leave the sea, and I am afraid of some \textit{[Greek: aristeia]} --- some heroic deed --- being done in my absence. Varro indeed disapproves of the boy's plan; I do not. If he has strong forces, he can have Brutus too --- and he is conducting his affairs in the open. He is enrolling at Capua, he is counting out the money. Already, already I see war. Reply to this. I am surprised that my courier set out from Rome on the Kalends without a letter from you.
