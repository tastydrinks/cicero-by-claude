\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} The moment your message touched my ears, Agamemnon --- not that I should come (for that too I should have done, were it not for Torquatus), but that I should write --- I dropped on the spot what I had begun, threw aside what I had in hand, and roughed out what you had ordered. You can learn the state of my accounts from Pollex. It is a disgrace, after all, that he, whatever sort he is, should be in want in this first year; later we shall keep a closer rein. Pollex himself must be sent back so that he may enter on the inheritance. There was plainly no going down to Puteoli, both for the reasons I have written you and because Caesar is at hand. Dolabella writes that he will be with me the day after the Ides. What a tiresome schoolmaster!
