\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} Quintus the father, for the fourth time --- or rather the thousandth --- shows no sense at all, rejoicing as he does in his son as Lupercus and in Statius, so that he may see his house piled high with a double disgrace. I add Philotimus as a third. What singular folly, were mine not greater! And what effrontery, to put the touch on you for a contribution \textit{[Greek: eranos]} toward this affair! Suppose he had come, not to some \textit{[Greek: dips\=osan kr\=en\=en]} (parched spring), but to \textit{[Greek: Peir\=en\=e]} (Pirene herself, the famous Corinthian fount) --- still, to draw upon you, who are \textit{[Greek: ampneuma semnon Alpheiou]} (the holy outbreath of Alpheus) in the spring, as you write, and that in such straits of his own as those --- \textit{[Greek: poi tauta ara]} (where, pray, can this be heading)?

\ciceroSection{2} But let him see to that himself. The Cato gives me pleasure, of course --- but his own Cato pleases Bassus Lucilius too.
