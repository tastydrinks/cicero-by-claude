\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} What incredible falsity! To his father, that he has to be out of the house on account of his mother; to his mother, full of devotion. And now the fellow is already going soft, and says his father is rightly angry with him.

\ciceroSection{2} But I will follow your advice; for I see you favour \textit{the crooked path} \textit{[Greek: skolia]}. To Rome, as you propose, I will come, though against my will: I am stuck fast in my writing. ``Brutus too,'' you say. Of course; but if it were not for this, the affair would not be forcing me. He has not come back from the place I would have wished, nor has he been away long, nor has he sent me so much as a single line. Still, I am eager to know how the whole tour has wound up for him. The books I wrote you about earlier --- please send them, and above all \textit{Phaedrus, On the Gods} \textit{[Greek: Phaidrou peri Theon]} and \dag\ \dag.
