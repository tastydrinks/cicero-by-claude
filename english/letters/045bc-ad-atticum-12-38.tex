\ciceroLetterOpener{CICERO ATTICO salutem}

\ciceroSection{1} I do not doubt that you have been extremely busy, since you sent me no letter; but the fellow is a knave, who would not wait on your convenience, when he had been sent off for that one reason. As things now stand, unless something has held you, I suspect you are at the place outside the city. As for me, here I find no relief at all in writing whole days, but at least I get out of myself a little.

\ciceroSection{2} Asinius Pollio has written to me about that impure kinsman of ours. Balbus the younger, recently, said it plainly enough; Dolabella obscurely; this man most openly. I should take it hard, were there any room left for fresh sorrow. But all the same --- could anything be more impure? What a man to be wary of! And yet for my part --- but the pain must be held in. Since there is no need, you will write me something only when you have leisure.
