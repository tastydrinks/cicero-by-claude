\ciceroLetterOpener{M. CICERO C. MVNATIO C. F. S.}

\ciceroSection{1} L. Livineius Trypho is in plain truth the freedman of L. Regulus, a very particular friend of mine, whose ruin makes me only the more attentive to the man --- for I cannot feel more goodwill towards him than I have always felt. But the freedman I love for his own sake, on his own account: for the services he rendered me were of the highest order in those times of ours in which it was easiest to gauge a man's true goodwill and good faith. I commend him to you on such terms as grateful and mindful men should commend those who have served them well. You will have done me the most welcome thing if he understands that what he did, in undertaking many perils for my welfare and many times sailing through the depth of winter, has earned him a return from you also, in keeping with the kindness you bear me.
