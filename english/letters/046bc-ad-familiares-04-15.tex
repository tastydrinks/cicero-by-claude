\ciceroLetterOpener{M. CICERO S. D. CN. PLANCIO.}

\ciceroSection{1} I have received your very brief letter. From it I could not learn what I wished to learn, though I did learn what I had never doubted: for how bravely you bear our common miseries, I did not gather; how much you love me, I saw at once. But the second I already knew; had I known the first, I would have shaped my own letter to fit it.

\ciceroSection{2} Even so --- though I have written before what I thought ought to be written --- I judged that at this moment I should remind you, briefly, not to suppose yourself in any plight that is yours alone. We are all in a great one, but in a common one. You ought not, then, either to claim some private and particular fortune of your own, or to refuse the common one. Therefore let our spirit toward each other be what it has always been; which on your side I can hope for, and on mine I can guarantee.
