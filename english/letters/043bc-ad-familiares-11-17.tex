\ciceroLetterOpener{M. CICERO D. BRVTO S. D.}

\ciceroSection{1} L. Lamia is the one man, of all men, with whom I am on the most intimate terms. The claims he has on me are great --- I will not say his good offices, but his deserts --- and they are very well known to the Roman people. He has discharged the office of aedile with the most magnificent show, and now stands for the praetorship, and everyone understands that he lacks neither the standing nor the popularity for it; but the canvassing seems to be stirred up in such a way that I am thoroughly alarmed for everything, and reckon that I must take Lamia's whole candidacy upon myself.

\ciceroSection{2} How far you can help me in this I see plainly enough; nor, in truth, do I doubt how far you wish to, for my sake. So I should like you to convince yourself of this, my Brutus: that there is nothing I ask of you with greater earnestness, nothing you could do that would please me more, than to support Lamia in his candidacy with all your resources, with all your zeal. And that you do so, I beg you most urgently.
