\ciceroLetterOpener{M. CICERO IMP. S. D. C. MARCELLO COS.}

\ciceroSection{1} How great a concern my distinction was to you, and how, as consul, you proved the very man in adorning and amplifying my standing that you had always been together with your parents and your whole house --- though the thing itself spoke for itself, I have learned in addition from the letters of all my friends. There is therefore nothing so great that I do not owe it on your account, and shall not perform [for you eagerly and gladly].

\ciceroSection{2} For it matters greatly to whom one is indebted, and I had rather be indebted to no one than to you. To you whom common pursuits, and your father's kindnesses and your own, had already bound me long since, there is now added what is, in my own judgment at least, the strongest bond of all: that you so conduct, and have conducted, that commonwealth which is dearer to me than anything, that, much as every loyal man owes you, I, for one, do not refuse to owe you no less than they. For that reason I would have such ends fall to you as you deserve and as I trust will be yours. As for me, if the voyage does not detain me --- which was running straight into the Etesian winds --- I shall, I hope, see you before long.
